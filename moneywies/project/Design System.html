§ 01

Principles

Four rules that govern every screen. When in doubt, choose the option that honors all four.

01
Numbers lead.

Money is the subject. Typography, alignment, and density should make the ledger legible at a glance.

02
One accent, used sparingly.

Forest green carries the brand. Reserve it for primary actions, active states, and moments that earn attention.

03
Warm, not sterile.

Cream paper over pure white. A personal ledger should feel like a private notebook, not a dashboard.

04
Banking-grade restraint.

No emoji, no exclamation marks, no cheerful persuasion. Dry, factual, accurate copy wins trust.

§ 07

Brand

The MoneyWies wordmark is set in Geist Medium at −0.03em. The mark is a rounded M in paper on forest. Voice is dry, precise, and quiet.

Voice
  • State the number, then the context. "$4,218 net in April, up 14%."
  • Never exclaim. Never cheer. No emoji.
  • Use active voice for actions: Import CSV, not CSVs can be imported.
  • Prefer exact numbers to vague adjectives. "12 transactions flagged" over "several".
Don't
  • Don't use emoji to convey status. Use pills.
  • Don't mix more than 2 chart colors unless you're showing categories.
  • Don't use pure white or pure black — respect the paper.
  • Don't decorate numbers. Tabular numerals and alignment are the decoration.
